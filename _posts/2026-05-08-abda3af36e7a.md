*TL;DR: Every multi-agent LLM architecture tested lost roughly a third of its output quality under semantic stress — yet every one also produced a statistical signal that a future learning system could exploit. CAFE is the measurement layer that tells the two apart.*

Take five multi-agent LLM architectures, hit them with contradictory evidence, overloaded context, ambiguous references, and stale data, and what happens?

Output quality drops by about a third. Every single architecture.

The ensemble — the one you'd expect to be most resilient — actually suffers the *largest* drop: 35.4%.

That looks like fragility. But de la Chica, Vera, and Rodríguez argue it's only half the picture.

The other half is a statistical signal hiding in the wreckage. The way stress deforms an architecture's response space carries structured information that no one is reading yet.

## Robustness Is Not the Only Question

Most evaluation of multi-agent LLM systems asks one thing: did performance survive the perturbation?

That is the robustness frame — fragile degrades, robust preserves.

But Taleb and Douady (2013) formalized a third regime: antifragility, where a system eventually benefits from adversity through convex exposure to dispersion.

CAFE — the Cognitive Antifragility Framework for Evaluation — translates that idea into a measurement for LLM architectures.

It does not claim the architectures *already* improve under stress. It asks a gentler, more useful question: does stress expose structured variation that an adaptive mechanism could later learn from?

## Four Stress Axes, Four Judge Signals

CAFE operates on a four-dimensional stress space. Each stressed prompt carries a vector

$$\psi = (\psi_{\text{conflict}},\; \psi_{\text{load}},\; \psi_{\text{ambiguity}},\; \psi_{\text{drift}})$$

where conflict injects contradictory banking-risk claims, load piles on extra ratios and caveats, ambiguity blurs entity references, and drift makes reporting windows stale or inconsistent.

A deterministic judge scores each response on four signals: coherence, grounded novel inference, contradiction resolution, and structural drift inverse.

These stay as a vector, not collapsed into a single quality score, because multiple signals constrain the reconstruction better than one.

## From Stress to Gap

Here is the mechanism in three steps.

**Step 1 — Fit a response surface.** For each architecture, a multi-output polynomial maps the four centered stress coordinates to the four judge signals. Linear terms capture direct sensitivity, quadratics capture curvature, bilinears capture cross-stressor interference, and two selected cubics let conflict modulate load and ambiguity modulate drift.

Ridge regularization keeps the fit honest.

**Step 2 — Invert to recover observed stress.** Given a judge signal vector, solve an inverse problem: which effective stress vector best explains this observation? The reconstruction uses an anchored regularizer and L-BFGS-B optimization, yielding an architecture-specific observed stress distribution $\hat{P}^{\text{obs}}_{\Psi,a}$.

**Step 3 — Compute the distributional Jensen Gap.** Under a convex potential $\phi(x) = \|x\|_2^2$, compute

$$G_a = \mathbb{E}[\phi(T_a(X))] - \phi(\mathbb{E}[T_a(X)]) - \left(\mathbb{E}[\phi(X)] - \phi(\mathbb{E}[X])\right)$$

A positive $G_a$ means the observed stress distribution has expanded relative to the designed one. Not better performance — richer stress geometry.

## Every Architecture, Positive Gap

The headline numbers are unmistakable.

All five architectures have positive distributional Jensen Gaps with bootstrap 95% confidence intervals strictly above zero. A4 Ensemble leads at 0.0975 [0.0822, 0.1146]. A2 Debate trails at 0.0780 [0.0647, 0.0927].

![Distributional Jensen Gap by architecture with bootstrap 95% confidence intervals, all above the resilience threshold]({{ "/assets/figures/abda3af36e7a/fig_007_01.png" | relative_url }})

Every confidence interval lies above the resilience threshold of 0.01.

And yet quality dropped for every architecture. Clean outputs scored 0.814–0.840; perturbed outputs scored 0.543–0.566.

![Clean versus perturbed quality scores across architectures, showing roughly one-third degradation under stress]({{ "/assets/figures/abda3af36e7a/fig_009_02.png" | relative_url }})

The antifragility-compatible ranking (A4 > A1 > A3 > A0 > A2) is *different* from a quality ranking. The ensemble that lost the most quality also exposes the most learnable structure.

## Architecture Writes Its Stress Signature

Different coordination mechanisms expand along different stress dimensions — and the pattern makes architectural sense.

A2 Debate expands most strongly along conflict ($\Delta\sigma$ = 0.0597). Of course it does: adversarial disagreement is designed to surface contradiction.

A3 Meta-Adaptive expands most strongly along drift ($\Delta\sigma$ = 0.0576), consistent with its coherence-and-drift monitoring role.

A4 Ensemble shows broad expansion across conflict, ambiguity, and drift — the diversity of independent analysts creates the widest stress footprint.

![Stress expansion signatures per architecture, showing how coordination mechanisms produce distinct expansion patterns across conflict, load, ambiguity, and drift]({{ "/assets/figures/abda3af36e7a/fig_010_03.png" | relative_url }})

Du et al. (2024) showed that multi-agent debate improves reasoning and factuality. CAFE complements that by identifying *which* coordination patterns expose learnable stress structure beyond immediate quality gains.

Shinn et al. (2023) showed that Reflexion agents can use verbal feedback to adapt across trials. CAFE targets the condition that precedes such adaptation — whether stress produces structured, convex-expansive variation in the first place.

## The Measurement, Not the Medicine

CAFE does not close the adaptation loop. It does not update memories, retrain routers, or switch architectures mid-deployment.

It tells you where the signal is.

That is its contribution and its limitation in one.

The response-surface fits are moderate (mean R² 0.45–0.49), making the reconstructed observed stress an effective latent quantity rather than ground truth.

The evaluation is single-domain — banking risk — and stress geometry may look very different in medicine or law.

The judge is deterministic but may carry rubric biases.

The honest reading: CAFE is a distributional diagnostic, not a per-sample oracle. Use it to decide where to attach adaptive mechanisms — stress-aware routing, memory updates, architecture selection — and then run the experiment that actually closes the loop.

## The Open Program

The paper opens a concrete research program: detect CAFE-positive stress modes, wire adaptive mechanisms into them, and measure whether repeated exposure turns opportunity into actual gains.

Under that program, the ensemble that cracks most under pressure is not the one you retire. It is the one you invest in — because its stress response is screaming with structure, waiting for something to learn from it.

Stress became signal. Now someone has to listen.
